If you've been watching the Flathead Valley market and wondering whether now is a smart time to make a move, here's the honest answer: it depends on which side of the transaction you're on — and most people are reading the headlines wrong.

Let's walk through the actual numbers, then talk about what they mean for you.

## The Numbers Right Now

Here's where the Kalispell market stands as of the June 2026 data (source: Movoto/MLS, June 2026):

- **Median list price in Kalispell: $627,495**
- **Price per square foot: $339**
- **Active listings: 732** — that's real selection for buyers
- **Median days on market: 58**, compared to 55 a year ago

Zoom out to the broader picture: Zillow puts the typical Kalispell home value at about $562,000 as of late May 2026, essentially flat — up just 0.2% over the past year. And for context, across greater Flathead County in 2025, single-family homes posted a median price of $689,000, with 1,238 sales — more transactions than the year before.

On the financing side, the 30-year fixed mortgage averaged **6.55%** for the week of July 16, 2026, per Freddie Mac.

## What This Actually Means

Here's the question worth asking yourself: *what does a flat market with growing inventory actually reward?*

It rewards preparation. Let me break that down for both sides.

### If You're Buying in Kalispell or Whitefish

With 732 active listings and homes taking nearly two months to go under contract, you have something buyers haven't had much of in the last five years: **leverage and time**.

Flat prices mean you're not chasing a runaway train. Sellers who've been sitting for 60+ days are negotiable — on price, on closing costs, on rate buydowns. A 1-point permanent buydown on a $560,000 purchase can matter more to your monthly payment than a $15,000 price cut. Most buyers never ask. The prepared ones do.

And if you're a veteran or active-duty service member: your VA loan benefit is one of the strongest financing tools in this market — zero down, no PMI, competitive rates. As an Army veteran myself, I walk fellow veterans through this one all the time, and it consistently surprises people how much buying power it unlocks here in the valley.

### If You're Selling

Read those days-on-market numbers again: 58 days median. The listings that sell fast in this market share three traits — they're priced to the data (not to what a neighbor got in 2022), they're prepared before they hit the MLS, and they're marketed with a plan rather than a prayer.

Overpricing in a flat market isn't ambitious. It's expensive. The market tells you within two weeks whether you missed, and chasing the price down afterward almost always nets less than pricing right on day one.

My approach comes from a background where planning wasn't optional: we brief the mission before we execute. Pricing strategy, prep checklist, marketing timeline, negotiation contingencies — mapped out before your sign goes in the yard.

### If You're Selling Under Complicated Circumstances

One more thing, because it doesn't get said enough: a meaningful share of home sales in any market happen because life changed — a divorce, a death in the family, an estate to settle. If that's your situation, the market data above still applies to you, but the *process* looks different. Timelines may be set by a court. Two parties may need to sign off on every decision. Discretion matters.

That's a lane I work in deliberately, with a process built for it. If that's where you are, you don't need a salesperson — you need a steady hand and a clear plan.

## The Bottom Line

The Flathead Valley isn't crashing and it isn't booming. It's normalizing — and normal markets reward the people who move with a plan instead of a hunch.
