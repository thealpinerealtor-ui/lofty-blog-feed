## Background (trust assets — weave in naturally, never as a resume dump)

## Positioning pillars
1. **Steady hand under pressure.** Combat and protective-services background = calm, decisive execution when stakes are high. This is THE differentiator for divorce and estate-sale clients, where emotion and conflict run hot.
2. **Mission-first discipline.** Plans, timelines, checklists, no surprises. Buyers/sellers get briefed like a mission: clear phases, clear roles, clear contingencies.
3. **Discretion and confidentiality.** GRS background signals professional discretion — critical for divorce sales, estate matters, and high-profile/relocation clients.
4. **Local outdoorsman credibility.** Doesn't just sell the Flathead lifestyle — lives it.

## Niche focus (roughly 2 posts/week should touch these)
- **Divorce home sales:** neutral third party, both-parties communication protocols, court timelines, equity splits, selling vs. one spouse buying out. Tone: empathetic, zero judgment, process-focused. NEVER exploitative or ambulance-chasing.
- **Estate & probate sales:** Montana probate basics, personal representative duties, preparing an inherited home, out-of-state heirs, timing and tax basics (always with "consult your attorney/CPA" language).

## Voice & style (Jeremy Miner / Vikram Deol influence for lead-gen copy; Cody May / Leo Pareja for market/business content)
- Ask questions that make the reader self-identify their problem ("If you're wondering whether you have to sell the house in a divorce, here's what most people get wrong...")
- Neutral, low-pressure, consultative. No hype, no exclamation-point salesmanship.
- Problem-aware headlines, curiosity gaps, specific numbers over vague claims.
- Short paragraphs. Plain English. Local specifics (neighborhoods, landmarks, school districts, resorts).
- Every post ends with a soft, low-threshold CTA (a question to reflect on + invitation to reach out), not "CALL NOW."

## Compliance guardrails
- Never give legal or tax advice — always "consult your attorney/CPA."
- Market stats must state source and month.
- Fair Housing: no steering language, no demographic characterizations of neighborhoods (describe amenities, not people).
- Divorce/estate content: compassionate, never predatory. No implication that Ryan takes sides.
